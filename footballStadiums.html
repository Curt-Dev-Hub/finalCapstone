TOP 10 FOOTBALL STADIUMS

Hi there! Welcome to this page.. Here we'll quickly go through the top 10 football stadiums in the world, this is based on atmosphere, capacity and visual design appearance. Enjoy!

10

Orange Velodrome - Marseille, France

Key facts Capacity: 67394 Opened - 1937

The new home of Olympique de Marseille. The stadium was almost completely rebuilt for the 1998 World Cup, and it was then when the stadium got its characteristic round-shaped stands. Capacity got increased to about 60,000 seats, though the new stadium was also criticised for its lack of cover.

9

Estadio Da Luz - Portugal, Lisbon

Key facts Capacity: 65,647 Opened - 2003

Portugal hosts a number of top European football clubs and their magnificent stadiums, but none compare to Benfica's Estadio da Luz. The work of art is the home of Portugal's most successful club and was inspiration for Arsenal's Emirates Stadium—the two stadiums designed by architectural firm Populous. A large capacity with an electric atmosphere and stunning architecture, there's nothing not to like about the Estadio da Luz..

8

First National Bank Stadium - South Africa, Johannesburg

Key facts Capacity: 94,736 Opened - 1989

Opened in 1989, the First National Bank Stadium—known as Soccer City—was renovated in 2009 ahead of the 2010 World Cup in South Africa. Although it is home to South African club side Kaizer Chiefs, the stadium is fondly remembered for hosting the showpiece event of the 2010 final, but also for hosting the opening game and goal of the tournament, Siphiwe Tshabalala finding the back of the Mexican net with an amazing goal. A truly wonderful football stadium.

7

Allianz Arena - Germany, Munich

Key facts Capacity: 71,437 Opened - 2005

The beautifully designed Allianz Arena is home to both Munich-based football teams: Bayern Munich and 1860 Munich. It is the only stadium across the Europe that is able to change colours to reflect which of the two teams are playing—red for Bayern and blue for 1860 Munich. The stadium has been a huge hit with both sets of fans thanks to the fans being closer to the pitch—something that caused an issue at the previous site, the Olympiastadion, which had a running track around the pitch that made for a rather poor atmosphere. The relatively new Allianz Arena will surely make a name for itself as one of the greatest stadiums in world football for years to come.

6

Tottenham Hotspur Stadium - England, London

Key facts Capacity: 62,850 Opened - 2019

Tottenham's new stadium, the aptly named Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, was a mighty task of footballing construction. But since it's opening, it has swiftly become one of the best respected and pleasant stadium experiences in the entirety of Europe.
The South Stand: Holding 17,500 fans, the seating bowl has put fans closer to the action than at any other stadium in the UK. The stadium was designed to produce an electric atmosphere with the fans very close to the pitch.

5

Camp Nou - Spain, Barcelona

Key facts Capacity: 98,757 Opened - 1957

The largest football stadium in Europe. The capacity of the Camp Nou once eclipsed 120,000 for the 1982 World Cup finals, but due to changes in laws regarding standing in stadiums, it has now been reduced. Some of the world's greatest footballers have graced the hollowed turf—most notably Diego Maradona and Lionel Messi, which is just another reason why the Camp Nou is one of the finest stadiums in world football.

4

Estadio Azteca - Mexico, Mexico City

Key facts Capacity: 105064 Opened - 1966

Mexico City's Estadio Azteca is renowned for its huge capacity and electric atmosphere when full, but it will go down in history as the only stadium to host two World Cup finals. Unfortunately for England fans, it is also the venue for Diego Maradona's infamous “Hand of God” goal. The venue may not be fondly remembered by the enormity of England fans, but there is no denying that the stadium—the third-largest football stadium in the world—is one of the finest around.

3

Wembley Stadium - England, London

Key facts Capacity: 67394 Opened - 1937

Wembley Stadium is undoubtedly the most iconic stadium in football. The new Wembley opened in 2007 was built on the site of the previous 1923 Wembley. Also known as The Home of Football.

2

Santiago Bernabeu - Spain, Madrid

Key facts Capacity: 85,454 Opened - 1947

Home to one of Europe's most succesful clubs, winning 10 European titles. Players like Ferenc Puskas, Luis Figo, Zinedine Zidane, Ronaldo, Cristiano Ronaldo, Gareth Bale, Luka Modric have all called they Bernabeu their home, while winning trophies.

1

The Maracana - Brazil, Rio de Janeiro

Key facts Capacity: 78,838 Opened - 1950

The stadium's popular name is derived from the Maracanã River, whose point of origin is in the jungle-covered hills to the west, crossing various bairros (neighborhoods) of Rio's Zona Norte (North Zone), such as Tijuca and São Cristóvão, via a drainage canal which features sloping sides constructed of concrete. Opened in 1950 after Brazil won the right to host the 1950 World Cup, an amazing stadium, home of arguably the greatest international Football team, situated in one of the worlds most iconic cities.
